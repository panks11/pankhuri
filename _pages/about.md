I'm an optimist who believes that good choices, made with intention and followed through with effort, lead somewhere worthwhile. My path has taken me through software engineering, finance, life sciences, and AI and ML research — each a deliberate step, each teaching me something the previous one couldn't. I love learning, and I especially love the moment when a research paper stops being theory and starts being something that solves a real problem.

I'm currently a Machine Learning Research Engineer at [Mindtrace](https://www.mindtrace.ai/) in Manchester, building agent-based AI systems for industrial asset inspection.

## My Journey

I began with a Bachelor's in Electronics and Communication Engineering from RGPV, India, and spent my early career as a software engineer — at BNP Paribas as a Python automation engineer for trading systems, then at IQVIA developing computer vision pipelines for life science document classification. Each role pushed me further into machine learning, until the ML work was the whole job.

I formalised that shift with a Post Graduate Program in AI and Machine Learning (2018–2019), and later completed an MSc in Artificial Intelligence at the University of Essex (2022–2023) — where I published my first research on federated learning for conversational AI. Since January 2023 I've been at Mindtrace, starting with computer vision and 3D deep learning for asset inspection, and now building the distributed agent systems that sit on top of that perception stack.
